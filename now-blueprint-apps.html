Speed-redesigning app deployment

A collaboration with Engineering to build powerful new workflows, empowering administrators to support their teams more efficiently. And making it look good on mobile, in German, to boot.

Collaborators
  • Ryan Rud, UI Engineer
  • Gary Flavin, UX Designer
Timeframe

2 weeks

Research

Lean Methodology; Feedback from Product, Customer Success, Support, Engineering.

Product

Jamf Now is a cloud-based Apple device management platform for small business. More than 20 million devices are managed on Jamf's platform.

The majority of our customers manage devices as a secondary or tertiary responsibility, so we focus on ensuring that anyone feels confident with our product, regardless of technical proficiency.

Our UX team uses five personas spanning all the levels of proficiency and responsibility we've come to know from our users.

Problems

Constrained UI

We needed to integrate new functionality into a workflow that administrators use to assign apps to devices. The thing is, this workflow was already stretched to its limits.

No space

Literally. We ran out of space on desktop, and the mobile experience was so overcrowded that it became practically unusable. We had to come up with a new, better experience, and fast. Our engineers had a two-week window to fit this in before they had to move on.

Not enough visual feedback

There were no cues as to which apps in the list were just added versus apps that had already been in the Blueprint.

No time!

Without time for an external usability study, we used lean methods to gather internal feedback from our customer-facing teams.

Process

I partnered with Ryan Rud, one of our UI Engineers, to work on an idea we had add and modify apps without modals. We jumped into Zoom calls, exchanged annotated mockups on Slack, and pulled in the other UI Engineers, Product Owners, Product Managers, fellow UX Designers, and our Jamf Now Customer Success and Support teams for feedback throughout.

The result was a workflow that everyone agreed was a pound-for-pound improvement over the existing. And we had plenty of time left to write up thorough design documentation for handoff.
